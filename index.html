About the Lake

One Lake. Endless Reasons to Come Back.

Straddling the South Carolina–Georgia border, Lake Hartwell is one of the most celebrated lakes in the American Southeast — a 56,000-acre reservoir with 962 miles of pristine shoreline, a legendary bass fishery, and a warmth that keeps families returning year after year.

The lake has hosted Bassmaster Classics, MLF Bass Pro Tour events, and collegiate championships that draw the world's best anglers. But it's equally beloved by families who come for the swimming coves, the boat days, and the kind of unhurried summer that feels rare everywhere else.

Our lakefront homes put you right in the middle of it all — private docks, sweeping water views, and everything you need to make the most of every day on the lake.

Species Guide

What's in the Water

Lake Hartwell is a multi-species fishery. Whether you're chasing tournament-grade largemouth, hard-fighting stripers, or a cooler of crappie for the fish fry, this lake delivers.

Signature Species

Largemouth Bass

The king of Hartwell. Legendary populations that attract top pros. Structure, docks, and creek channels hold fish year-round. Prime in spring spawn and fall feed-up.

→ Best: March–May spawn, Sept–Nov fall
Tournament Target

Spotted Bass

Hartwell's "spots" stack up in deep, clear water and hit finesse presentations hard. Drop shots, shaky heads, and Ned rigs excel.

→ Best: Deep structure year-round, finesse tactics
Trophy Fish

Striped Bass

The SC state record — 59+ lbs — came from Hartwell. Stripers roam main lake channels following blueback herring and shad. Live bait and big swimbaits are the ticket.

→ Best: Spring and fall, main lake channels
Hard Fighter

Hybrid Striped Bass

Pound-for-pound the most exciting fight in Hartwell. "Wipers" boil on baitfish at the surface in fall mornings and hit spoons aggressively. Abundant and willing.

→ Best: Fall topwater at daybreak, spoons & jigs
Family Favorite

Crappie & Bream

Excellent crappie around brush piles, docks, and timber. Spring spawns are productive off points and in coves. Perfect for kids and light-tackle days off the dock.

→ Best: Spring spawn, small jigs and minnows
Night Fishing

Catfish

Channel and flathead cats are abundant in Hartwell's deep holes and river channels. Night fishing from the dock with cut bait produces consistently all summer.

→ Best: Summer nights, deep holes and channels

Monthly Fishing Tips — July 2026

What's Working Right Now

Full summer on Hartwell. The spawn is long done and bass have grouped up offshore — points, humps, brush, and timber — feeding on blueback herring. The early and late bite is best; the middle of the day goes deep. Here's exactly what to throw, where, and on what setup — with rod recommendations from our partners at Denali Rods.

July First Light

Topwater — Walking Baits & Flukes

Heddon Zara Spook / SPRO McStick / Zoom Super Fluke (herring colors)
Where: Long main-lake points, humps, and open-water bait schools in the first two hours of daylight. Spots crush walking baits chasing blueback herring — cast to surface activity and keep it moving.
Denali Recommendation
Denali AttaX Pro 7'3" M Casting
15lb fluoro or 30lb braid. Softer tip lets you work the walking cadence and load up on long casts.
"July mornings on Hartwell are all about the herring bite. Get on a long point at first light with a walking bait and hang on — the spots are vicious this time of year." — Guide Rick Dodd
July Midday

Drop Shot — Offshore Brush & Timber

Roboworm Straight Tail / Zoom Finesse Worm / Strike King Dream Shot
Where: Offshore brush piles, standing timber, and deep points in 15–35ft once the sun climbs. Idle and watch your graph — mark the fish and the bait first, then drop straight on them.
Denali Recommendation
Denali Kovert Lite 7'0" ML Spinning
8lb Seaguar Tatsu fluorocarbon, 1/4oz weight. Extra-sensitive blank is critical for those deep summer feels.
"When July sun gets high, the fish pull out to the deep brush and timber. A drop shot dead on their heads is hard to beat — spots and largemouth both stack up out there." — Guide Mike Harter
July — Docks & Shade

Shaky Head — Deep Dock Fishing

Zoom Trick Worm / NetBait Tiny Paca Craw / Strike King Dream Shot
Where: Deep docks in the mouths of coves and on main-lake pockets. In July the fish hold on the deepest posts and brush under the dock — skip into the shade and let it fall on a semi-slack line. The bite is almost always on the drop.
Denali Recommendation
Denali AttaX Pro 6'10" L Spinning
10lb fluorocarbon, 3/16oz head. Light enough to skip deep under docks, sensitive enough to feel the dock-post bite.
"Dock fishing on Hartwell in July is money — the shade is the only cool water they've got. Skip that shaky head as far back and as deep as you can. The biggest fish live in the shadiest corner."
July — Offshore Schools

Swimbait — Herring Schools & Humps

Keitech Swing Impact FAT 4.8" / Zoom Swimmer / Berkley PowerBait Hollow Belly (herring/shad)
Where: Offshore humps, long points, and channel-swing schools in 12–25ft where bait is showing on the graph. Count it down to the fish and slow-roll it through the school.
Denali Recommendation
Denali Lithium Pro 7'6" H Swimbait Casting
15lb Seaguar InvizX fluorocarbon. Slow, steady retrieves produce — watch for the line to jump on the strike.
"A swimbait through an offshore herring school in July catches the biggest ones of the day. Once you find one hump loaded with bait, you can sit on it all morning."
July — Night Catfish

Cut Bait — Catfish off the Dock

Cut shad, chicken liver, or blue gill. Find the deepest water you can reach from the dock.
Where: Cast off the dock into the channel after dark. Fish on the bottom with a slip sinker rig, 2–4oz weight depending on current. Warm July nights are prime — the deeper the better.
Denali Recommendation
Denali Gunner 7'0" MH Casting
30lb mono or braided, #4/0 circle hook. Durable and affordable — perfect for dock night fishing.
"July night fishing is the best way to beat the heat. Channel cats bite hard from the dock after 9pm — set your rods in holders and enjoy the cool night. They'll let you know."
July — Hybrids & Stripers

Topwater Spook — Wiper Blowups

Heddon Super Spook / Zara Spook Jr / Chug'n Spook. White or chrome.
Where: Main-lake points and open water at first light. In July the hybrids and stripers chase herring to the surface — watch for the boil and gulls, then cast into it immediately. A cast net of live herring fished on a downline is the deep-water backup once the sun is up.
Denali Recommendation
Denali AttaX Pro 7'3" M Casting
15lb fluoro or 30lb braid. Hybrids fight hard — don't undergun this bite.
"When the wipers blow up on a July morning it's absolutely electric. Keep a walking bait in your hand every second you're on open water — that window is short and it's worth it."
